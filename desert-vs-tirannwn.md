# Desert vs Tirannwn — The 3rd Region Decision

**Strategy:** Pick Kandarin and Kourend first (1st and 2nd picks). Make Desert vs Tirannwn the **3rd and final region choice** so you can verify mechanics in-game before committing.

This decision is genuinely close and depends on what content you want to prioritize. Both builds work. This doc lays out the full trade-off so you can decide informed.

---

## Why This Goes Last

By the time you're picking your 3rd region:
- You'll have tested Devil's Element's "30% universal weakness" claim in-game
- You'll have tested whether SQ counts as a "Magic powered weapon" for crystal blessing
- You'll know if Culling Spree is actually unlocked and Echo Thermy is feasible
- You'll have farmed CoX a bit and know if Ancestral pieces are dropping
- You'll have farmed Doom of Mokhaiotl and have Eye of Ayak + Avernic Treads
- You'll have a clear picture of what content you actually enjoy in the league
- You can read community posts about which builds are working vs theorized

This is the highest-leverage region decision in the build. **Don't lock it in early.**

## The Two Builds Side by Side

### Option A: Crystal Tribrid Mage (Tirannwn)

**Regions:** Varlamore + Karamja + Kandarin + Kourend + **Tirannwn**

**Build identity:** Crystal armor + crystal blessing as the universal accuracy/damage backbone. Pure mage pacts. Tribrid via gear swaps (Macuahuitl melee, Bowfa ranged, EoA mage) without spending any non-mage pact points.

**Primary weapon:** Eye of Ayak + F8 → 1-tick (~28 DPS with crystal set bonus)

**Flex weapons:**
- Macuahuitl (melee for Sol Heredit, magic-resistant content)
- Bowfa (ranged for ranged-mandatory content)
- SQ + DE (Slayer AoE backup, no crystal benefit)

### Option B: ToA Mage (Desert)

**Regions:** Varlamore + Karamja + Kandarin + Kourend + **Desert**

**Build identity:** Traditional mage build. Ancestral / Virtus mage armor with Eye of Ayak / Tumeken's Shadow as primary weapons. Lightbearer for spec management. ToA as endgame raid content.

**Primary weapon:** Eye of Ayak + F8 (1-tick) OR Tumeken's Shadow + F8 (2-tick)

**Mage armor options:**
- Ancestral (CoX, Kourend) — bonus drops
- Virtus (DT2 bosses... wait, those need Desert? Actually Vardorvis is Varlamore. Other DT2 bosses need other regions. Limited Virtus access.)
- Mystic / Blue Moon set as fallback

## The Decision Criteria

### Pick Tirannwn If:

- ✅ You enjoy CG and want to farm crystal armor + blessing
- ✅ Tribrid flexibility (one armor set for melee/mage/ranged) appeals to you
- ✅ You want Bowfa as a ranged option without farming Masori
- ✅ Prifddinas Agility/Thieving QoL matters
- ✅ You want Zulrah as a consistent GP/uniques boss
- ✅ You're OK with rune pickaxe permanently (~6 hrs slower mining)
- ✅ You don't care about ToA (or you've done it before in mainscape)
- ✅ Crystal armor's set bonus on EoA validates the build for you

### Pick Desert If:

- ✅ ToA is your favorite raid and you want to push high invocations
- ✅ Tumeken's Shadow + crystal armor pairing appeals (wait, you can't have both — Tumeken's is Desert, crystal is Tirannwn)
- ✅ Lightbearer ring is critical for your spec management strategy
- ✅ You want dragon pickaxe + dragon harpoon for skilling tool completionism
- ✅ Tempoross fishing minigame is fun for you (Spirit Flakes are nice)
- ✅ Pure mage focus without tribrid flexibility is fine
- ✅ Giants' Foundry Smithing is preferred over anvil (despite being functionally similar)
- ✅ You don't want to farm CG or you don't enjoy it

### The Critical Question: Did the DE/L1 Mechanics Pan Out?

**If Devil's Element's "30% universal weakness" works as the wiki text says:**
- The HeavyNettle air mage build (max prayer + SQ + L1 + I1) becomes universally BIS
- Crystal armor matters less because air mage out-DPSes EoA on everything
- **Either region works** because the air mage build doesn't need crystal armor OR ToA
- 3rd region becomes pure content preference

**If DE only doubles existing weaknesses (Raging Echoes behavior):**
- Air mage is specialized for natively-air-weak content only
- Single-target bossing falls back to EoA + crystal (Tirannwn) or EoA + Ancestral (Desert)
- Tirannwn becomes more valuable because crystal blessing > Ancestral for EoA setups
- 3rd region matters more

**Test on Day 1-2 before committing:**
1. Equip SQ + DE
2. Cast Air Surge on a non-air-weak target
3. Compare max hit vs no DE
4. If +30% damage → DE works as universal weakness → either region fine, lean Tirannwn
5. If same damage → DE only doubles existing → Tirannwn for crystal armor matters more

## Final Verdict (Revisable on Day 1-2)

**Lean: Tirannwn.** Reasons:

1. **Build identity:** Crystal armor + blessing is unique and creates a coherent tribrid build. ToA is great content but doesn't change your core combat approach.

2. **Mining tool penalty is small:** ~6 hours total league time for d-pick speed loss. Not deal-breaking.

3. **CG is content you enjoy:** This matters for an 8-week league where you'll spend hundreds of hours playing. Prefer content you like.

4. **Zulrah/Prifddinas/Zalcano fill the content gap left by no ToA:** You're not bored without Desert.

5. **Bowfa as backup ranged option:** Without spending any ranged pacts, you have a top-tier ranged weapon for melee-mandatory content. Tribrid flexibility for free.

6. **Mage damage stack equivalence:** Crystal armor + blessing + EoA + F8 = ~28 DPS. Ancestral + EoA + F8 = ~25 DPS. Crystal wins on raw mage damage too.

**Counter-argument:** If you want to push ToA high invocations and farm Tumeken's Shadow / Lightbearer / Masori, Desert is the only path. ToA is a top-3 raid in OSRS and the rewards are excellent.

**Decide based on:** what you'd rather do 50+ times in the league — CG farming runs OR ToA raid runs.

## Reset #2 / Reset #3 Considerations

If you pick wrong, you can't reset regions. But you can:
- Reset pacts (3x per league) to optimize for the gear you actually have
- Adjust gear loadouts based on drops
- Adapt strategy based on what's working

The region pick is permanent. **Get it right by waiting until you have maximum information.** That's why this is the last region pick.
