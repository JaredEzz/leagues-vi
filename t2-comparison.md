# Tier 2 Comparison: Hotfoot vs Woodsman vs Friendly Forager

**Context:** This analysis assumes EH (T1) + Flow State (T7) are locked. The question is which T2 relic maximizes the triple synergy.

**Current pick: Hotfoot.** But this is the closest decision in the build.

---

## The Three Options

| Relic | Core Effects |
|---|---|
| **Hotfoot** | Searing boots: auto-cook caught fish, auto-smelt mined ore (coal-free). Passive Agility XP while running (every 10 ticks, scales with Agi level). 100% Agility + Cooking success. Double Agility course completions + 25% bonus XP. Guaranteed marks of grace. |
| **Woodsman** | Chopped logs auto-burn for full FM XP. Hunter: 100% success, 2x loot/XP, auto herb/tree seed drops from traps. Implings drop 2x noted loot. Fletching processes 10x simultaneously (stackable). Rumour loot sacks 2x. |
| **Friendly Forager** | Forager's pouch: finds herbs while gathering. 90% Herblore secondary ingredient save. 4-dose potions (not 3). Herblore batch processing. |

## The Agility Question

> "I can endlessly harvest barbarian fishing for unlimited agility afk xp"

This is true — **all three T2 options get passive Agility XP from EH barb fishing** (Agi + Str XP per catch, 2-tick from Flow State = 2.5x the passive Agi/Str rate).

But barb fishing Agility XP is small: ~10-15k Agi XP/hr base from barb fishing (varies by level). At 16x = ~160-240k/hr. That's decent but slow — 99 Agility needs ~13M XP, so barb fishing alone = ~54-81 hrs of fishing to max Agility as a side effect. You'd hit 200m Fishing XP long before 99 Agility.

**Hotfoot adds on top of barb fishing Agi XP:**
- Passive running Agi XP (always active, not just while fishing)
- 100% Agility course success (no fails at high courses = way more XP/hr)
- Double course completions + 25% bonus XP = ~2.25x Agility course XP/hr
- Ardougne rooftop at 2.25x: ~70k base × 2.25 = ~157.5k/hr → **~2.52M/hr at 16x** (vs ~1.12M/hr without Hotfoot)

**Without Hotfoot, Agility is the slowest 99 in the build.** The skill guide says ~14 hrs base, ~10 hrs at Ardougne. With Hotfoot's 2.25x course boost, it drops to ~4.5 hrs at Ardougne.

Without Hotfoot (Woodsman or FF), Agility is:
- ~10 hrs at Ardougne rooftop (90+ real, no Abundance +10)
- Plus passive barb fishing trickle — maybe shaves 1-2 hrs off if you fish a LOT

**Hotfoot saves ~5-7 hrs on Agility** beyond what barb fishing provides.

## The Smithing Question

> "mining gems and calcified rocks for crafting and prayer"

Gem rocks and Calcified Rocks are **not affected by T2 choice** — all three options are equal. These are pure Mining + EH + Flow State synergies. The T2 relic only matters for ore-based mining.

But ore-based mining with Hotfoot is where the coal-free auto-smelt changes everything:

**With Hotfoot:** Mine runite → 2x rune bars auto-smelted (coal-free) → auto-banked → smith platelegs → alch for 38,400 gp each. ~4.5-6.4M GP/hr AFK.

**Without Hotfoot:** Mine runite → 2x raw runite ore → need to buy 8 coal per ore (360 gp) → smelt at furnace (separate trip) → then smith + alch. Same GP but way more steps, requires coal purchase, requires furnace trips. Not truly AFK.

### Qualitative factors

**Hotfoot's advantages aren't just time savings:**
1. **Coal-free auto-smelt is irreplaceable.** No other relic, pact, or item gives this. Without it, ore mining + smelting is fundamentally clunkier.
2. **Cooking being free means more Fishing AFK time.** You're not splitting gathering time between "fish for Fishing XP" and "cook for Cooking XP" — they're the same activity.
3. **Passive running Agility XP is always on.** Every time you run anywhere in the league, you earn Agility XP. This adds up over 8 weeks.
4. **100% Cooking success** means no burnt food. Every auto-cooked fish is Cooking XP. This matters for low Cooking levels where burn rates are high.

**Woodsman's advantages:**
1. **Hunter is a stated hard grind** and Woodsman gives the biggest improvement to it. 4x Hunter XP/hr (with Flow State) vs 2x (without Woodsman) is a real difference.
2. **FM being free during WC** is nice, but you're doing WT anyway for points/clog. The FM savings are partially redundant.
3. **Fletching 10x batch** is powerful but Fletching is already fast at 2-tick.
4. **2x Rumour loot sacks** = better Hunter contract rewards.

## What About Wintertodt?

The user said they're doing WT for points and collection log. This affects the Woodsman comparison:

- **With Woodsman:** FM is free during WC → you do WT purely for points/clog/supply crates, not FM XP. You might still do 50-100+ WT games for rewards.
- **Without Woodsman:** FM trains at WT (~2.5 hrs to 99 at 16x). You're doing WT anyway for points/clog, so FM is essentially solved either way.

**Woodsman's FM savings are largely redundant** with a WT plan. This narrows the gap — Woodsman's real value is Hunter + Fletching, not FM.

## Verdict

**Hotfoot wins** for this build, but it's close. Here's why:

1. **Coal-free auto-smelt is build-defining.** It enables the AFK runite GP loop, eliminates coal as a resource concern, and makes ore mining a dual-skill activity. Nothing else provides this.
2. **Cooking being free saves more time than FM being free** (3.5 hrs vs 2.5 hrs), and FM is already covered by WT.
3. **Agility savings are huge** — the difference between a ~10 hr grind and a ~4.5 hr grind. Barb fishing Agi XP helps but doesn't come close to replacing Hotfoot's course boosts.
4. **Hunter's Woodsman boost (4x vs 2x XP/hr) is the best argument against Hotfoot.** But Hunter at 2x (Flow State only) is still fast — ~4.5 hrs to 99, which is manageable.
5. **Fletching at 2-tick without batch processing is still ~2-4 hrs** — annoying but not a dealbreaker.

### When to reconsider

Switch to Woodsman if:
- Hunter turns out to be way harder than expected (locked creatures, bad rumour RNG)
- A pact or other mechanic provides coal-free smelting or auto-cooking
- You decide not to do WT at all (making FM savings from Woodsman non-redundant)
- Fletching supply becomes a bottleneck

Switch to Friendly Forager if:
- Herblore secondary supply becomes the single biggest bottleneck in the league
- A pact provides auto-cooking and/or coal-free smelting (making Hotfoot's main perks redundant)

**Current pick: Hotfoot.**
